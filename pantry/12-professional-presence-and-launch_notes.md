## A. Conceptual foundations

### Launch as coordinated proof

Launch is the moment when the artifact, brand strategy, visual identity, story, portfolio, resume, and public profile must agree. The goal is not noise; it is coherent proof directed at a specific audience.

**Common misconception:** Launch means posting everywhere. Better: launch means the right audience can understand and act on the proof.

**Worked example:** A LinkedIn post, portfolio update, pitch deck, and resume all point to the same flagship AI project and claim.

**Source(s):** TIKTOC.md; local `_lib_How_to_Deliver_a_TED_Talk.md`.

### Pitch discipline

Guy Kawasaki's 10/20/30 rule offers a useful constraint: 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30-point font. Its value is compression and audience cognition, not ritual.

**Common misconception:** A pitch deck should include everything. The deck should force selection.

**Worked example:** A self-pitch deck: problem, audience, artifact, proof, differentiation, roadmap, ask.

**Source(s):** Guy Kawasaki https://guykawasaki.com/the-only-10-slides-you-need-in-your-pitch/

### Launch proof and startup clarity

Airbnb's 2009 pitch deck is often used as a clarity case: a simple marketplace problem, solution, market, traction, model, and team story. Use it to teach simplicity and evidence.

**Source(s):** Airbnb history seed funding summary https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Airbnb; pitch analysis source https://www.pitchai.com/sample-pitches/airbnb-seed-2009

## B. Domain examples and cases

### Case: Airbnb seed deck

Use to show simple proof architecture: problem, solution, traction, model, team. Verify exact slide count and funding details in final manuscript.

### Case: LinkedIn as professional presence

LinkedIn is a professional network where profile coherence, public proof, and network signaling matter. Use current research carefully and avoid overclaiming causal effects.

**Source:** LinkedIn profile research preprint https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.12905; LinkedIn professional network context https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.02268

### Failure case: Launch with inconsistent assets

A resume claims product strategy, portfolio shows only code, LinkedIn says AI researcher, pitch says founder. The audience cannot locate the person.
