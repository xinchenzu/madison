The slideument problem

A slide built for two audiences serves neither.

Open on the experience, not the definition: you built a deck at midnight; the room read it instead of listening. Ask: whose attention was the slide asking for? Then name the term — slideument — only after they feel the problem.

Background

Reynolds coined "slideument" in 2005; the cognitive basis is Mayer's Redundancy Principle.

Interrogating the model matches writing it yourself.

The variable is checking, not typing.
Hand-coding and AI-plus-interrogation land together; delegation drops off a cliff. The differentiator is cognitive engagement. Walk the three bars, then state the claim: checking, not typing.
