Services are acts a consumer is willing to pay for, and there are
hundreds of different types. One such service is day care. According to
the US Chamber of Commerce, 88 percent of two-parent families and 83
percent of single-parent families relied on non-parental care prior to
the COVID-19 pandemic and spent approximately \$42 million on early care
and education.

Sara Bullock, who has a background in early childhood education and four
children of her own, owned a childcare business in Maryland for more
than 10 years. When her husband was required to move to Tennessee for
his job, she closed her business and started a new childcare business
using a different business model. She changed it from a conventional
daycare center to a "drop-in" center, allowing parents to bring in their
children for short periods of time (anywhere from one to seven hours)
without requiring them to make a monthly commitment. That was the birth
of MeTime Drop-In Child Care. The concept has been so successful that
Bullock has already begun plans to open a second location.
