# Chapter 29 — Case: PULSARAI — The Pulse of Tech Intelligence

*A tech-news intelligence startup brand built around a single radical-affordability commitment — three minutes and one penny per run replaces two hours and forty dollars per day per knowledge worker — anchored on a Creator × Magician archetype pairing, a pricing model published on the deck, and a fully-shipped five-platform brand ecosystem.*

## Situation

Tech professionals in 2026 spend approximately two hours each morning manually scanning tech news across a dozen browser tabs — RSS readers, Slack feeds, Twitter, Substack inboxes, vendor blogs. The deck reports the structural cost: *87% of tech professionals miss critical trends — buried in information overload* [verify primary source for the 87% figure]; *$40/day in lost productivity per employee on manual monitoring*. Existing solutions either aggregate without analysis (Feedly at $8/mo, Techmeme free, raw RSS readers) or analyze without making the analysis accessible (AYLIEN at $500-plus per month, API-only, requiring developer integration). The brand task is to build the no-code platform that combines AI sentiment analysis, automated trend detection, and ready-to-post social content generation into a single workflow priced for radical affordability — and to argue the business case explicitly: *3 seconds and $0.01 replaces 2+ hours and $40/day — a 99.9% cost reduction in intelligence gathering*. The Creator × Magician archetype pairing is named on the deck and is doing real strategic work — the brand promises both *building something visible* (Creator) and *making complex AI invisible to the user* (Magician).

## Architecture

The pipeline is a five-stage automation composed end-to-end in approximately three minutes per run at a per-run cost of one cent. **Stage 1 — Ingest.** RSS feed ingestion across 42-plus articles from configured top tech sources [verify source list]. **Stage 2 — Analyze.** GPT-4o-mini scores sentiment, detects trends, classifies categories, extracts topics, and assesses business impact — eight named AI analyses per article. **Stage 3 — Report.** Interactive Streamlit dashboard renders sentiment-distribution pie chart, category-breakdown bar chart, trend-signals detection, and article-analysis table. **Stage 4 — Create.** Auto-generation of ready-to-post Twitter and LinkedIn social content from the analyzed signal set — the *output-oriented* pillar made operational. **Stage 5 — Orchestrate.** n8n connects the RSS ingestion to the AI analysis layer to the Streamlit output layer without requiring engineering teams or hand-built integration; the *no-code* commitment is what lets the radical-affordability claim hold.

The brand surfaces are unusually fully-shipped at the cohort level. The [marketing site at pratiksha-pulsarai.netlify.app](https://pratiksha-pulsarai.netlify.app) is live with eight named sections (Hero, Problem, How It Works, Features, Competition, Pricing, Story, Contact). The live application at [tech-intelligence-app-9mrugahgm529cxmjlrsmr8.streamlit.app](https://tech-intelligence-app-9mrugahgm529cxmjlrsmr8.streamlit.app) is the working tool a presenter can demo and an investor can try without an account. The [Substack at pulsarai.substack.com](https://pulsarai.substack.com) carries the long-form thought-leadership voice. The [LinkedIn at pratikshamohod](https://www.linkedin.com/in/pratikshamohod) hosts the brand's primary professional surface. The [Medium discovery channel](https://medium.com/p/749a039cb22e) extends reach into the developer-and-product-manager audience. Five platforms, all live, all aligned to the same Brown / Gold / Violet visual system.

## Design rationale

The architectural commitment that earns the brand its name is **radical affordability rendered as architectural choice, not as marketing claim**. The deck's pricing math is concrete: GPT-4o-mini at the per-token cost the model card publishes, scoped to the configured 42-article workflow, hits roughly $0.01 per full intelligence run. The architecture is what makes the price defensible — n8n orchestration eliminates engineering integration cost; Streamlit eliminates frontend-build cost; GPT-4o-mini's specific price point (rather than GPT-4 or Claude Sonnet) is the deliberate model-selection decision that holds the per-run cost at one penny. The competitive table on the deck makes the same argument quantitatively: AYLIEN at $500-plus per month with no-code-not-supported, PULSARAI at *Free* tier with no-code-yes. The radical-affordability commitment is therefore an architectural-decision artifact, not a discount.

The **Creator × Magician archetype pairing** is the second consequential design move. Creator carries the *building something visible* register — five shipped platforms, eight site sections, the explicit deliverable count. Magician carries the *making complex AI invisible to the user* register — eight AI analyses per article rendered as a single dashboard the user does not need to inspect to use. The pairing is doing real differentiated work: Creator alone would land as builder-vanity (look at all the platforms I shipped); Magician alone would land as opaque (the AI does its work, trust me); together they argue *I shipped the visible thing AND made the underlying complexity invisible to the user it is for*. The brand's *No-Code Accessibility* pillar operationalizes the Magician half; the *Output-Oriented* pillar operationalizes the Creator half.

The **WCAG-AAA contrast discipline** is the third consequential design move. The deck reports the visual system's contrast ratios explicitly: Gold-on-Brown at 8:1, White-on-Brown at 19:1, both well above the WCAG AAA threshold of 7:1. Most cohort startup decks gesture at accessibility; this deck commits to it numerically and uses the discipline as part of the visual system's argument. The Brown / Gold / Violet palette is unusual for an AI-tool startup brand (the cohort default is cool tech blue), and the warm-palette choice plus the WCAG-AAA contrast discipline distinguishes the brand from the AYLIEN-and-Feedly visual register the user encounters when shopping the category.

## Trade-offs

The radical-affordability claim depends on GPT-4o-mini's per-token pricing remaining stable and on the 42-article scope holding. A model-version change that increases the per-token cost or a feature-creep decision that doubles the article scope would push the per-run cost above the $0.01 anchor and damage the brand's central differentiator. The fix at the architecture level is explicit cost-budget gating in the n8n workflow that surfaces a *cost exceeded — review configuration* state before silent overruns. The 87%-of-tech-professionals-miss-trends figure is the brand's headline pain anchor and would benefit from a primary-source citation on the marketing site rather than appearing as an ambient statistic. The Year-1 ARR target ($174K from 500 Pro users at $29/mo) and the $50K-seed-for-3.5x-return ask are appropriately scoped at the early-stage-pitch level; the customer-acquisition discipline that produces those 500 Pro users is the next-revision frontier and depends on the LinkedIn-and-Substack-and-Medium content cadence demonstrating sustained reach beyond the academic submission deadline. The five-platform shipping count is unusually high for one semester — the discipline that prevents post-deadline platform-decay (every platform requires ongoing content to remain credible) is the second consequential post-submission target.

## Outcomes and revisions

The brand is shipped at the level of artifacts an early-stage investor or design-partner customer can verify. Marketing site at pratiksha-pulsarai.netlify.app live with all eight sections executed. Live application at the streamlit.app endpoint demonstrating end-to-end the 42-article ingestion, GPT-4o-mini analysis, Streamlit dashboard output, and auto-generated social content. Substack at pulsarai.substack.com publishing under the brand voice. LinkedIn optimized with branded banner and hexagonal logo. Medium presence extending reach. Three pricing tiers published — Free at $0/mo, Pro at $29/mo, Enterprise at $149/mo — with named feature differentiation across each tier. The development roadmap is appropriately staged: Q2 2026 (user auth, email digests, custom RSS feeds, Product Hunt launch), Q3-Q4 2026 (Pro and Enterprise tiers, team dashboards, developer API, mobile responsive). Both Q2 and Q3-Q4 commitments are next-stage work, not shipped — and the deck names them explicitly as such, which is consistent with the case template's *honest reporting* requirement. Market-test outcomes — Pro-tier conversions, design-partner enterprise customers — are not reported here.
