# Chapter 16 — Case: Production AI Systems Specialist

*A research-credentials-meet-production-engineering personal brand built around a single defining metric — 150 seconds to 28 seconds, a 81% latency reduction on a real legal-AI platform — with PositionPulse as the flagship demonstrating the same systems-thinking move at the product level.*

**Author:** Swara Kamal Joshi
**Editor:** Nik Bear Brown

---

## Situation

A spring 2026 Northeastern MS candidate carrying three IEEE / ICASISCT / ACIT publications, one patent, and an AI-startup internship at Crossing faced a positioning problem the chapter calls out specifically: the *research engineer* lane and the *production engineer* lane are usually held by different people, and the candidate who carries both is the candidate the market is willing to pay a premium for — provided the brand makes the combination legible. The *AI/ML Engineer* market is saturated with researchers who have not deployed and with engineers who have not published. The brand task was to occupy the seam without the seam reading as a hedge. The defining moment the brand uses to anchor the position is the Crossing project: an AI legal platform processing documents at 150 seconds each, with 60% of the time spent in user-perceived wait and 25% of API calls redundant; the engineer redesigned the architecture (asynchronous processing, queuing, rate limits) and brought the per-document time to 28 seconds — an 81% reduction. The number is the brand's central artifact. The story it carries is the brand's central argument.

## Architecture

The brand resolves to a positioning sentence with a deliberate exclusion clause: *I build production AI systems that work within real-world constraints — not just in demos*. The UVP names the rare quadrant: *I don't just build AI systems — I make them production-ready*. Five pillars carry the load curve. **Research-Backed.** Three publications and one patent supplying theoretical depth that informs design decisions. **Production-Proven.** Crossing internship, 81% faster systems, 20+ APIs shipped. **Full-Stack Capable.** FastAPI, PostgreSQL, Docker, React, AWS (EC2, RDS, S3, Lambda) named explicitly rather than gestured at. **AI/ML Specialized.** LangChain, RAG, CrewAI, LoRA fine-tuning, multi-agent systems, LLMOps as the working vocabulary. **Systems Thinker.** The discipline that connects the other four — *optimizes architecture, not just algorithms; builds for real users, not benchmarks*.

The visual identity is calibrated to read as Linear-Stripe-Vercel-adjacent rather than ambient-AI-marketing. Primary blue `#3b82f6` for trust signaling, slate dark `#0f172a` for headers and body text, success green `#10b981` for positive metrics (the 81% number, the throughput claims), and neutral grey `#64748b` for secondary elements. Inter Bold at 36–48pt for headings, Inter Regular at 24–30pt for body, JetBrains Mono for code. The style note on the deck names the references explicitly: *Linear, Stripe, Vercel*. The palette discipline matches; the brand reads as the same family of considered-technical aesthetics those three companies established in product marketing.

The flagship project is **PositionPulse** — a competitive-research tool that analyzes 100+ brands instantly through five-minute analyses, identifies positioning gaps and strategic opportunities, and is priced at $49–99/month for SMB customers locked out of the $20K+/year enterprise tools and underserved by the surface-level free options. The deck reports manual competitive research as a 40-hour activity replaced by a five-minute one — a 480× compression — and frames the product through the same systems-thinking move: *identify the constraint, redesign the architecture, ship something real*. The Crossing-and-PositionPulse pairing is the brand's structural argument made twice. The first time the move was applied to a workflow inside someone else's product. The second time the move *is* the product.

The deployment surfaces are [swarajoshi-portfolio.vercel.app](https://swarajoshi-portfolio.vercel.app) [verify] (the central hub), [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/swarajoshi) [verify], a [Substack](https://swarajoshi1.substack.com) [verify], and joshi.swar@northeastern.edu as the canonical contact. The brand is positioned for *AI/ML Engineer at product-focused companies, Backend Engineer at AI-first startups, Founding Engineer opportunities*.

## Design rationale

The architectural commitment that earns this brand its name is **the rare-quadrant positioning carried at the artifact level, not the slogan level**. The UVP claims a quadrant most engineers don't occupy — *research depth + production rigor* — and the brand's evidence load is calibrated to the claim. Research depth is not asserted; it is anchored to three named venues (IEEE, ICASISCT, ACIT) and a patent. Production rigor is not asserted; it is anchored to a numbered Crossing outcome (150s → 28s, 81% reduction, 20+ APIs shipped) and a deployed flagship (PositionPulse). The pillar structure is built so that no single pillar can be challenged without being challenged on a verifiable artifact. That is the discipline Chapter 13's *metrics-as-positioning* argument applied with a publishing record on top.

The **Linear-Stripe-Vercel aesthetic move** is the second consequential design choice. The default for AI-engineering portfolios is one of two failure modes — too much marketing color (which reads as overcompensating) or too little visual structure (which reads as not having thought about it). The Linear-Stripe-Vercel reference set is the established register that says *I take craft seriously and I do not need to be loud about it*. The palette discipline (one accent blue, one success green, one slate, one neutral) and the typographic discipline (Inter at three weights plus JetBrains Mono for code) execute the register without copying any one of the references. The audience that matters — engineering hiring managers at product-focused companies — reads the visual system as legible craft.

The **single-defining-metric framing** is the third design move. The Hero's Journey brand story does not load five metrics across five sections. It loads one — 150s to 28s, 81% — and uses the structure to extract a portable principle: *production AI is built on constraints, not capabilities*. The principle is what the engineer carries forward into every system the brand subsequently describes. PositionPulse exists because the principle was applied to a product-shaped problem; the *what would change my mind* test on the brand is whether a third application of the same move would deepen the argument or expose its limit.

## Trade-offs

The research-plus-production positioning is calibrated tightly. The brand reads as authoritative to AI/ML engineering hiring managers; it reads as over-credentialed for pure-startup Founding Engineer roles where publication record is parsed as overhead and as under-research-y for pure-research roles where 81% latency reduction is a category error. The five-pillar maximalism risks the *pillar fatigue* that minimal-pillar brands deliberately avoid; the discipline is that each pillar attaches to a verifiable artifact, which keeps the load supported. The single-defining-metric move is brittle in the same way Abhishek Prakash's brand is brittle: the 150s → 28s number must be defensible under questioning about baselines, sample sizes, and the specific call paths optimized. The PositionPulse pricing claim ($49–99/mo) does customer-acquisition work the brand cannot yet anchor with active-customer numbers, which is fine at submission and becomes the next-revision frontier for the post-graduation period.

## Outcomes and revisions

The brand is shipped at the level of artifacts a hiring manager can verify. Portfolio at swarajoshi-portfolio.vercel.app [verify], LinkedIn optimized, Substack published, GitHub populated. The Crossing internship outcome (150s → 28s on the legal-AI platform) is the headline number; the deck does not document the throughput context — concurrent users, document-size distribution, hardware envelope — and that documentation is the most consequential next-revision target for a recruiter who clicks through. PositionPulse is reported as a working flagship with a stated SMB price point; live-site URL, customer count, and funnel metrics are the post-submission hardening work. Three publications and one patent are listed by name; the citations and patent number would benefit from being visible at the portfolio surface. Market-test outcomes — interviews, offers, role acceptance — are not reported here; the brand is shipped, the test runs in the months following graduation.
