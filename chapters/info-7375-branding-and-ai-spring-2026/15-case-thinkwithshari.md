# Chapter 15 — Case: thinkwithshari

*An AI-engineering personal brand built around the move from reactive data analyst to proactive systems builder — proof-over-claims as the load-bearing pillar, with three deployed agentic systems on the portfolio shelf.*

**Author:** Mycline Shareena John Peter Kennedy
**Editor:** Nik Bear Brown

---

## Situation

A spring 2026 Northeastern MS Information Systems candidate carrying eighteen months of ETL-and-data-infrastructure work at BankBazaar plus an earlier role at SMRC Automotive faced the labor market the chapter argued she would face: 2024's job-market verdict had been *strong analyst, limited engineering proof*; applications met silence; the roles she wanted demanded *deployed projects that did not yet exist*. The brand task was specific. It was not to position as a data analyst — that lane she could already speak from. It was to credibly position into AI engineering, where the gap between *I know the techniques* and *I have shipped a system that uses them in production* was the gap most candidates failed to close. The strategic answer was to stop applying and start building. The brand the deck describes is the artifact of that decision.

## Architecture

The brand resolves to a single positioning sentence — *I close the gap between raw data and production AI* — and an UVP that names the architectural register of the work: *architect agentic systems that plan, execute, evaluate, and self-correct autonomously*. The brand is published under a wordmark, *thinkwithshari*, that doubles as the public identity (vercel domain, Substack handle, GitHub presence). Four pillars carry the load curve. **Build in public.** Document the journey, share the work, let the process speak. **Proof over claims.** Every claim attaches to a number traceable to a deployed system: 60% modeling-time reduction, 70% faster research cycles, 1,000+ concepts retrieved at scale. **Systems thinking.** The reactive-to-proactive transition framed as architectural commitment, not aspiration. **Authenticity (technical, outcome-driven).** Brand essence stated as *Specific. Technical. Outcome-driven.* — three words functioning as a do/don't filter on every piece of content the brand publishes.

The visual identity is a four-color palette named for the brand: *Shari Ink* (`#0A2342`, navy authority), *Shari Turquoise* (`#00B4D8`, precision/innovation), *Shari Amber* (`#F4A261`, warmth/energy), *Shari Parchment* (`#F5F0E8`, paper background). Headings in DM Sans Bold; body in Inter. The wordmark renders *thinkwith* in Parchment and *shari* in Turquoise — clean, asymmetric, deliberately not-a-monogram. The three-color triangulation (navy + turquoise + amber) puts technical authority and human warmth in the same frame, which is the synthesis the brand argues — *bridging data and human connection*.

The portfolio shelf is what makes the brand defensible rather than aspirational. Three deployed agentic systems carry the weight: **Prompt2Mesh** (autonomous build with self-correction), **ORBIT** (orchestrated AI workflows), and **AURELIA** (production-ready agentic systems). The flagship for the final presentation is **Prismly** — a competitive-intelligence tool that ingests competitor RSS feeds and surfaces brand archetypes, emotional tone, and strategic shifts. The reported performance is concrete and unusual in its honesty: *102 articles processed in 5 minutes at $0.08 total cost, 100% success rate, 90% confidence in brand analysis*. The stack is GPT-4o-mini for reasoning, n8n on Render for orchestration, Streamlit for the front-end. Live at [prismly.streamlit.app](https://prismly.streamlit.app) [verify].

The deployment surfaces are [thinkwithshari.vercel.app](https://thinkwithshari.vercel.app) [verify] (the central hub with case studies, architecture diagrams, and downloadable resumes), an optimized [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/myclineshareena), [GitHub/MyclineShareena](https://github.com/MyclineShareena) (proof artifacts at the source), and [myclineshareena.substack.com](https://myclineshareena.substack.com). The Substack debut is reported as 517 impressions on the first article with engagement from a *top AI voice* in the comments [verify identity and metric source]. The brand consistency audit on the deck shows the navy-turquoise-amber-parchment palette executed identically across portfolio header, LinkedIn banner, and Substack header.

## Design rationale

The architectural commitment that earns this brand its name is **proof-over-claims as deduplication discipline**. The deck reports two specific moments where the discipline did real work. First, it reframed the application strategy: the brand stopped sending applications and started shipping systems, on the premise that a deployed agentic system with measurable outcomes would do work no resume bullet could. Second, it produced a closed feedback loop — the author ran her own LinkedIn content through Prismly, and the analysis flagged that her posts focused heavily on *how* she built things and rarely on *why* it matters, which inspired a Substack article on authentic technical storytelling. Prismly didn't only analyze competitors; it surfaced the blind spot in the brand's own voice. That recursive use of the tool — *the brand turns the brand's own tool on the brand* — is the move that makes the *Build in public* and *Proof over claims* pillars cohere as practice rather than slogan.

The **three-color triangulation** is the second consequential design move. A two-color palette (navy + accent) is the conventional move for a technical-credentials brand and reads as competent but indistinguishable. The amber third color is the move that visually argues the *bridging* claim — navy authority meets turquoise precision meets amber warmth, and the bridge is rendered in palette before it is rendered in copy. The risk is amber over-running into a register that reads as marketing-warm; the discipline that prevents it is the parchment background, which keeps the color load light enough that amber stays as accent.

The **sequenced project shelf** is the third design move. Prompt2Mesh, ORBIT, AURELIA, and Prismly are not four equally-weighted portfolio tiles. Prismly is the flagship the deck demonstrates live; the other three are the architecture the flagship sits on top of. The story the portfolio tells in linear order is *I built the agentic foundations first, and Prismly is the application layer the foundations enable*. The hiring manager who reads four projects as four projects sees a generalist; the hiring manager who reads four projects as a sequence sees an architect.

## Trade-offs

The proof-over-claims discipline is brittle in the same way Chapter 13's metrics-first brand is brittle: every number must be traceable, and the *517 impressions* and *90% confidence* claims require the deck-linked evidence to be visible at the portfolio surface, not only on the slide. The Sage-Creator-leaning aesthetic and the *systems builder* vocabulary deliberately filter the audience toward AI-engineering and data-engineering hiring managers; a recruiter screening for analyst or BI-developer roles would read the brand as out-of-lane. The brand depends on the four shipped systems remaining live and accessible — when streamlit.app endpoints sleep or n8n workflows on Render time out, the brand's most defensible artifacts go quiet, and the portfolio needs a degraded-mode story (screenshots, recorded walkthroughs, source-linked READMEs) for the periods when live demos are unavailable.

## Outcomes and revisions

The brand is shipped at the level of artifacts a hiring manager can verify. Portfolio at thinkwithshari.vercel.app [verify], LinkedIn optimized with banner and featured content, GitHub populated with the four named projects, Substack debut measured (517 impressions on first article [verify]). Prismly is reported live at prismly.streamlit.app with the 102-articles-in-5-minutes figure as the headline metric. The Northeastern faculty endorsement (Professor Nik Bear Brown and Professor Nina Harris on LinkedIn) is reported as social-proof signal. The market-test outcomes — interviews, offers, role acceptance — are not reported here. The 90%-confidence figure on Prismly's brand analysis would benefit from explicit documentation of the validation set: how many brands were analyzed, what the ground truth was, who labeled it.

## Pattern connection

The case instantiates Chapter 1's argument that legibility is the binding constraint on the labor market in 2026 — *thinkwithshari* is the legibility move executed at the wordmark level. It instantiates Chapter 8's brand-strategy synthesis (UVP, four pillars, two named target personas — *Alex the AI Engineering Lead* and *Maya the Data Engineering Manager* — and a positioning statement). It instantiates Chapter 9's visual-identity-as-strategy commitment with the unusually deliberate three-color triangulation. It instantiates Chapter 10's storytelling work (Hero's Journey + Overcoming the Monster, with the *2024 application silence* as the explicit monster). And it instantiates Chapter 11's portfolio-as-product framing — Prismly is the product the portfolio is about, with the other three systems as the architectural foundation that makes the product credible.
